# **RIMC Reference Bibliography**

*A Curated Map of Theoretical Lineage and Structural Influences*

| Category                                    | Author & Title                                                                                                                                           | Year        | Relevance to RIMC / Structural Influence                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ------------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Economic & Financial Theory**             | George Soros, *The Alchemy of Finance*                                                                                                                   | 1987        | Foundational text on **reflexivity**. Provides the philosophical starting point for shifting psychological reflexivity into **technological–capital recursion** within RIMC.                                                                                                                |
| **Economic & Financial Theory**             | Eugene F. Fama, *Efficient Capital Markets: A Review of Theory and Empirical Work*                                                                       | 1970        | Core EMH reference. In RIMC, the assumption of “instant information incorporation” is reformulated into a **finite-time information-lag structure**.                                                                                                                                        |
| **Economic & Financial Theory**             | William F. Sharpe, *Capital Asset Prices: A Theory of Market Equilibrium under Conditions of Risk*                                                       | 1964        | CAPM origin. Serves as the departure point for Chapter 6, where CAPM is extended from a **static equilibrium** to a **delay-driven dynamic equilibrium** framework.                                                                                                                         |
| **Growth & Technological Evolution Theory** | Joseph A. Schumpeter, *The Theory of Economic Development*                                                                                               | 1934        | Prototype of recursive innovation: **technology creates capital, and capital accelerates technology** — the conceptual backbone of the $R$–$V$ loop in RIMC.                                                                                                                                |
| **Growth & Technological Evolution Theory** | Philippe Aghion & Peter Howitt, *Endogenous Growth Theory*                                                                                               | 1998        | Coupled dynamic models of innovation and growth. Provides the formal skeleton for the **joint differential system** linking technological recursion $R$ and economic value $V$ in RIMC.                                                                                                     |
| **Nonlinear & Dynamic Systems Theory**      | Alfred J. Lotka, *Elements of Physical Biology* / Vito Volterra, “Fluctuations in the Abundance of a Species Considered Mathematically” (*Nature*, 1926) | 1925 / 1926 | Classical predator–prey system. Mathematical ancestor of RIMC’s two-variable coupled ODEs governing $R$ and $V$.                                                                                                                                                                            |
| **Nonlinear & Dynamic Systems Theory**      | Richard Bellman, *Adaptive Control Processes: A Guided Tour*                                                                                             | 1961        | Adaptive control theory. Provides a near-isomorphic structure to $$\frac{d\alpha}{dt} = \varepsilon_R G - \lambda \alpha,$$ including theoretical grounding for the **forgetting rate** $\lambda$. Approximate structural correspondence: **95%**.                                          |
| **Nonlinear & Dynamic Systems Theory**      | Yoshiki Kuramoto, *Chemical Oscillations, Waves, and Turbulence*                                                                                         | 1984        | Phase-synchronization model (Kuramoto Equation). Offers the mathematical prototype for multi-observer interference terms such as $$\sum_{j\neq i}\kappa_{ij}\sin(\alpha_j - \alpha_i).$$ Approximate correspondence: **90%**. Defines RIMC’s multilayer $\alpha$-interference architecture. |
| **Information, Control & Systems Theory**   | Claude E. Shannon, *A Mathematical Theory of Communication*                                                                                              | 1948        | Origin of information-delay and noise theory. Provides formal justification for the disturbance term $\eta(t)$ and the decay kernel $e^{-\lambda(t-\tau)}$ in RIMC.                                                                                                                         |
| **Information, Control & Systems Theory**   | Norbert Wiener, *Cybernetics: Or Control and Communication in the Animal and the Machine*                                                                | 1948        | Foundational framework for feedback and control. Structural basis of RIMC’s recursive and delay-stability properties.                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Information, Control & Systems Theory**   | Rudolf E. Kalman, *A New Approach to Linear Filtering and Prediction Problems*                                                                           | 1960        | Linear filtering theory. Formal ancestor of the information-update and decay mechanisms underlying RIMC’s $\alpha$-drift equation, specifically the interpretation of the forgetting rate $\lambda$.                                                                                        |
